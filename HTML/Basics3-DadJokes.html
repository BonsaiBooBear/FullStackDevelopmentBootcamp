Dad Joke Epidemic Addressed by UN

REPORTS ARE COMING IN FROM AROUND THE WORLD: The number of delivered Dad Jokes has reached record-breaking highs, with no signs of slowing down. The United Nations Secretary-General called an emergency assembly this morning to address the crisis at hand, and a bold and actionable solution was proposed.

The Nature of the Dad Joke

Dad jokes are typically characterized by their ability to elicit audible groans through use of wordplay. Some are even considered anti-jokes, and derive humor from intentionally non-humorous punchlines.

Delivered by fathers, father figures, and the occasional rogue uncle, these jokes are typically followed by the phrase: "Haha, get it?"

Societal Impact

While seemingly harmless, dad jokes have been known to cause temporary embarrassment, forced laughter, and in extreme cases, immunity to humor. Shocking first-hand accounts, like those below, were considered during the UN Emergency Assembly.

NOTE: Sources are kept anonymous due to privacy and protection laws.

Middle School Daughter "He just wouldn't stop! I had friends over for dinner, and I almost DIED from embarrassment! Cringe was served as a main course, and it was horrifying."
High School Junior "Dude, the teachers just don't know when to quit. If I have I have to hear 'I used to be a baker, but I couldn't make enough dough' one more time, I'm gonna hurl."

The Proposal

In order to preserve the delicate balance of humor and dignity, it has been proposed that dad jokes be formally recognized as a critically endangered form of comedic expression. A new licensing system shall be introduced, wherein individuals must pass a Pun Proficiency Qualification Exam before delivering jokes such as:

"What do you call a flock of birds that stick together? Vel-crows!"

Illegal punning, or telling dad jokes without an valid license, will result in a $5,000 fine and up to 6 years in federal imprisonment.

Implementation Strategy

A new law will be introduced to regulate Dad Jokes. It will be known as D-Law. Under D-Law:

  1. Jokes MUST be approved by a locally certified Groan Council.
  2. Joke submissions must be received by the council 24 hours prior to desired date of joke delivery.
  3. If approved, the applicant will receive a temporary "Dad License," valid for 3 days.
  4. The applicant will then be allowed to perform in a designated Dad Joke Delivery Zone. These zones will be established in public parks, hardware stores, and car dealerships.

Violaters of D-Law who misuse puns in unauthorized contexts will be subject to:

  • Up to a $5,000 fine
  • Up to 30 days in Dad Jail
  • Up to 9 months of Community Service
    Note: Will include reading knock-knock jokes to toddlers until they giggle.

Conclusion

Though dangerous if unregulated, dad jokes have become a vital part of our cultural fabric. With the proper regulation, training, and respect this proposal introduces, future generations just may be able to experience the joy of hearing:

“What's brown and sticky? A stick.”
